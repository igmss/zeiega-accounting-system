4. The Manufacturing Workflow — Day by Day

This is the core flow. Follow it for every customer order.

  1. Customer Places Order Enter manually or receive automatically from your e-commerce website
  2. Create Sales Order Operations > Sales Orders → + Manual Order. Select customer, add design items, set sale price. Status: Pending
  3. Start Production Click ▶ on the pending order. A Work Order is created automatically. Status: Producing
  4. Issue Materials to WIP Operations > Work Orders → click 🔧 icon. Add materials, labor hours, labor cost, overhead. Behind the scenes: value moves from Raw Materials (1201) to WIP (1710-1712)
  5. Apply Labor & Overhead Record worker time and indirect cost allocation through the same update dialog
  6. Complete Production Click ✓ on the work order. Costs transfer from WIP to Finished Goods (1220). Status: Completed
  7. Create Invoice Finance > Invoices. Creates journal: Debit AR (1110) / Credit Sales Revenue (4003) + COGS (5301)
  8. Record Payment Finance > Payments. Creates journal: Debit Cash/Bank (1101/1103) / Credit AR (1110)

The order is now complete — revenue recognized, costs tracked, profit visible in reports.

Income Statement Structure

Revenue
  + Product Sales - Retail (4001)
  + Product Sales - Wholesale (4002)
  + Custom MTO Orders (4003)
  + Online Sales (4011)
  - Sales Returns (4091) / Discounts (4090)
  = Net Revenue

Cost of Goods Sold
  + Raw Materials Used (5001)
  + Direct Labor (5002)
  + Manufacturing Overhead (5004)
  = Total COGS

GROSS PROFIT = Net Revenue - COGS

Operating Expenses
  + Salaries (6001) + Rent (6002) + Marketing (6101) + Depreciation (6007) ...
  = Total OpEx

OPERATING INCOME = Gross Profit - OpEx
NET INCOME = Operating Income ± Other Items

Balance Sheet Structure

ASSETS
  Current: Cash (1101-1107) + AR (1110) + Inventory (1201-1240) + Prepaid (1130)
  Fixed: Equipment (1301-1307) - Accum Dep (1351-1354)
  = Total Assets

LIABILITIES
  Current: AP (2101) + VAT Payable (2110) + Accrued (2140)
  Long-term: Loans (2201-2210)
  = Total Liabilities

EQUITY
  Partner Capital (3011-3013) + Retained Earnings (3100) + Current P&L (3200)
  = Total Equity

Total Assets MUST EQUAL Total Liabilities + Equity

7. Year-End Close

Path: System > Year-End Close — Accountant or Admin only. Do once per year.

The close calculates net income, transfers it to Retained Earnings (3100), resets revenue/expense accounts to zero, and creates closing entries.

Before Closing Checklist
  • All transactions for the year are recorded
  • Bank accounts are reconciled
  • Inventory has been counted and adjusted
  • All invoices are issued
  • All payments are recorded
  • Depreciation is recorded for the year
  • Reports have been reviewed and approved

After closing, closed-period entries cannot be modified, protecting your audited financials.

9. Best Practices & Tips

Daily Routine

  1. Morning: Check dashboard KPIs and pending orders
  2. During day: Create sales orders; start production on pending
  3. End of day: Record all payments received and materials issued
  4. Weekly: Review AR Aging — follow up on overdue payments
  5. Monthly: Run Income Statement and Balance Sheet; review profitability

Data Entry Tips

TipWhy
Always select a design when creating ordersEnsures accurate cost tracking and margin calculation
Record materials as they’re issuedKeeps WIP valuation accurate in real-time
Update labor costs per work orderPrevents understated production costs
Reconcile bank monthlyCatch discrepancies early
Enter expenses promptlyAvoid missing deductions at tax time
Use descriptive notes on journal entriesMakes audits much easier

True Production Cost

Direct Materials (fabric, buttons, zippers)
+ Direct Labor (worker wages for that order)
+ Manufacturing Overhead (share of rent, utilities, maintenance)
= Total Production Cost

Track all three for accurate job profitability. Skipping overhead or labor makes profit look higher than reality.

Inventory Management

  • Count inventory monthly — compare physical to system
  • Record adjustments via Inventory > Adjust
  • Track scrap separately (account 1205)
  • System uses FIFO (First-In-First-Out) — oldest stock used first

10. Troubleshooting

“I can’t see a module in the sidebar”

Your role limits visibility. Ask admin to adjust your role. Viewers only see Dashboard and Reports.

“The balance sheet doesn’t balance”

Check: (1) Opening balances Unallocated Difference was zero. (2) Run Trial Balance first — if balanced, reconstruct Balance Sheet step by step.

“I created a sales order but no work order appeared”

Work orders are only created when you click ▶ Start Production. Pending orders don’t have work orders yet.

“My inventory quantity is wrong”
  1. Check Inventory > Inventory Movements
  2. Verify all material issuances and PO receipts were recorded
  3. Use Inventory > Adjust to correct
“The dashboard shows no data”

Click Refresh. Ensure you have opening balances, at least one customer, and one sales order. New systems start with zeros.

11. Glossary of Accounting Terms

TermSimple Definition
Accounts Payable (AP)Money you owe suppliers
Accounts Receivable (AR)Money customers owe you
Accrued ExpenseExpense used but not yet paid (e.g., electricity bill received later)
AssetSomething you own with value (cash, inventory, equipment)
Balance SheetSnapshot: what you own vs what you owe
COGMCost of Goods Manufactured — total to make finished products
COGSCost of Goods Sold — cost of products actually sold/delivered
Contra AccountAccount that reduces another (e.g., Accumulated Depreciation)
CreditRight side — increases revenue, liabilities, equity
DebitLeft side — increases assets, expenses
DepreciationSpreading asset cost over useful life (10K machine / 5 yrs = 2K/yr)
Double-EntryEvery transaction hits ≥2 accounts; debits always = credits
EquityOwner/partner stake = Assets − Liabilities
FIFOFirst-In, First-Out inventory costing (oldest stock sold first)
Gross ProfitRevenue minus COGS (before operating expenses)
Journal EntryRecord of a transaction with debited and credited accounts
LiabilitySomething you owe (loans, payables, taxes)
Net IncomeRevenue minus ALL expenses — the bottom line
OverheadIndirect production costs (rent, utilities, maintenance)
POHRPredetermined Overhead Rate — formula allocating overhead per job
Retained EarningsAccumulated profits from prior years, reinvested
RevenueMoney earned from selling products/services
Trial BalanceAll accounts with balances — debits must equal credits
WIPWork In Progress — partially completed goods in production
VATValue Added Tax — collected from customers, paid to authority
